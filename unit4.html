Lesson #6 Conditionals Investigate C.F.U

When creating an if-else-if statement you should always make your first condition the most specific. Write a short paragraph responding to the questions below. What does it mean to put the most specific case first? Why is it important to put the most specific case first? What types of errors does it help avoid?

Initially, the term "most specific case" refers to the most selective part of the if-else-if statement or the case that serves as a guide for determining the other cases. Because the computer executes code top to bottom, it is crucial to start with the most specific case because anyplace else but the top will result in serious bugs.
